A smartphone-only Parkinson’s self-management app for non-motor symptom tracking, medication-effect logging, cognitive check-ins, and caregiver support.

Parkinson’s Disease

This project proposes a smartphone-only Parkinson’s app focused on daily support, not just symptom recording. It helps users track non-motor symptoms such as sleep, mood, fatigue, constipation, pain, and swallowing difficulty, while also linking those symptoms with medication timing, simple cognitive check-ins, and caregiver support.

Project Summary
ParkinsonCare+ is designed for people with Parkinson’s disease who may have different education levels, different digital skills, and different levels of independence. The app includes a daily check-in module, cognitive mini-games, a medication reminder and response log, weekly symptom trend reports, and a caregiver mode for assisted use.

Its main novelty is that it combines several features that are usually separated in existing apps:

non-motor symptom monitoring
post-medication effect tracking
simple cognitive assessment
caregiver-assisted reporting
mixed-literacy accessibility
Core Idea
The app works in a simple daily routine:

The user checks in symptoms like sleep, pain, fatigue, constipation, mood, and swallowing difficulty.
The app asks how the person feels after taking medicine.
Short mini-games measure memory, attention, or reaction time.
Weekly reports show symptom patterns in easy graphs.
A caregiver can enter data when the patient cannot use the app alone.
